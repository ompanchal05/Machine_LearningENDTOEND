STATISTICS FOR MACHINE LEARNING (DEEP + EASY)
=============================================

PURPOSE
-------

This document is created for:
- Strong ML foundation
- Interview preparation
- Long-term (365-day) learning
- Data Analyst → Data Scientist → ML Engineer

Language: Very simple English  
Focus: Understanding, not memorization  
Rule: Statistics explains WHY ML works

=================================================
SECTION 1: WHAT IS STATISTICS?
=================================================

Q1. What is Statistics?
A:
Statistics is the study of data.
It helps us:
- Understand data
- Find patterns
- Make decisions

-------------------------------------------------

Q2. Why statistics is important for ML?
A:
Because ML models are built on:
- Probability
- Distributions
- Data patterns
Without statistics, ML is guessing.

=================================================
SECTION 2: TYPES OF STATISTICS
=================================================

Q3. Types of statistics?
A:
1. Descriptive Statistics
2. Inferential Statistics

-------------------------------------------------

Q4. What is Descriptive Statistics?
A:
It summarizes data.

Examples:
- Mean
- Median
- Mode
- Variance

-------------------------------------------------

Q5. What is Inferential Statistics?
A:
It helps to make conclusions about population using sample data.

Examples:
- Hypothesis testing
- Confidence intervals

=================================================
SECTION 3: MEASURES OF CENTRAL TENDENCY
=================================================

Q6. What is Mean?
A:
Mean is the average.

Formula:
Sum of values / Number of values

-------------------------------------------------

Q7. What is Median?
A:
Median is the middle value after sorting data.

-------------------------------------------------

Q8. What is Mode?
A:
Mode is the most frequent value.

-------------------------------------------------

Q9. When to use mean vs median?
A:
- Mean → when data has no outliers
- Median → when data has outliers

=================================================
SECTION 4: MEASURES OF SPREAD
=================================================

Q10. What is Range?
A:
Range = Max value − Min value

-------------------------------------------------

Q11. What is Variance?
A:
Variance shows how much data spreads from the mean.

-------------------------------------------------

Q12. What is Standard Deviation?
A:
Standard deviation is the square root of variance.
It shows average distance from mean.

-------------------------------------------------

Q13. Why standard deviation is important?
A:
It tells how consistent or unstable data is.

=================================================
SECTION 5: PROBABILITY (ML FOUNDATION)
=================================================

Q14. What is Probability?
A:
Probability measures chance of an event.

Range:
0 to 1

-------------------------------------------------

Q15. What is conditional probability?
A:
Probability of event A given B already happened.

-------------------------------------------------

Q16. Why probability matters in ML?
A:
ML models predict likelihood, not certainty.

=================================================
SECTION 6: DISTRIBUTIONS
=================================================

Q17. What is a distribution?
A:
It shows how data is spread.

-------------------------------------------------

Q18. What is Normal Distribution?
A:
A bell-shaped curve where:
- Mean = Median = Mode

-------------------------------------------------

Q19. Why normal distribution is important?
A:
Many real-world data follows it.
Many ML models assume it.

-------------------------------------------------

Q20. What is skewness?
A:
Skewness shows if data is left-skewed or right-skewed.

=================================================
SECTION 7: CORRELATION & RELATIONSHIP
=================================================

Q21. What is Correlation?
A:
Correlation shows relationship between two variables.

-------------------------------------------------

Q22. Types of correlation?
A:
- Positive
- Negative
- No correlation

-------------------------------------------------

Q23. Correlation vs Causation?
A:
Correlation does NOT mean causation.
One variable may not cause the other.

=================================================
SECTION 8: HYPOTHESIS TESTING
=================================================

Q24. What is hypothesis?
A:
A statement to test.

-------------------------------------------------

Q25. Null hypothesis (H0)?
A:
No effect or no difference.

-------------------------------------------------

Q26. Alternative hypothesis (H1)?
A:
There is an effect or difference.

-------------------------------------------------

Q27. What is p-value?
A:
p-value tells probability of result happening by chance.

-------------------------------------------------

Q28. p-value rule?
A:
- p < 0.05 → significant
- p ≥ 0.05 → not significant

=================================================
SECTION 9: STATISTICS IN MACHINE LEARNING
=================================================

Q29. Where statistics is used in ML?
A:
- Loss functions
- Model assumptions
- Evaluation metrics
- Feature selection

-------------------------------------------------

Q30. Why mean squared error is used?
A:
Because it penalizes large errors more.

-------------------------------------------------

Q31. Why log loss is used?
A:
Because it works with probabilities.

=================================================
SECTION 10: STATISTICS FOR INTERVIEWS
=================================================

Q32. Why median preferred in skewed data?
A:
Because it is not affected by outliers.

-------------------------------------------------

Q33. What happens if variance is high?
A:
Data is widely spread.

-------------------------------------------------

Q34. Why correlation is important?
A:
To understand feature relationships.
